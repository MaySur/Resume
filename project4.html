WCU Exhibit Website

Website

About Project:

This website serves the West Chester University's Museum of Anthropology and Archaeology's new exhibition, "Surrounded By The Spirits". It involved collaboration with an interviewer, video editor, and website creator.

MacLaren Remy, a History major with a passion for Anthropology, conducted four engaging Zoom sessions with Dr. Paul Stoller. These sessions unearthed valuable narratives about the West African Songhay community's cultural richness and traditions.

Harrison Warren, a graduate in Urban & Environmental Planning, expertly edited the recorded videos, extracting key moments and creating shorter clips. These clips were uploaded to YouTube to reach a broader audience, with final touches by Ryan Riley.

I developed the website using React.JS, delivering the interview content in an easy and interactive manner for the exhibit's audience. Visitors can explore Dr. Paul Stoller's work and engage with the Oral History application dynamically. The site's user-friendly design accommodates different learning styles, making it ideal for distance learning and promoting accessibility.

I used React.JS to make the website more interactive and modular, ensuring compatibility with various screen sizes. Additionally, I designed it to be accessible to all viewers, minimizing difficulties for website visitors.

Furthermore, I utilized the GitHub Pages platform to host the website, providing an easily accessible link that is live and linked to the university's main website.
